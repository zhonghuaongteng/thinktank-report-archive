# NIH科研人员流失：经费变化下美国实验室的人才压力

## 中文摘要与研判

ITIF引用一项针对1000名NIH资助研究人员的调查：13%的受访者表示，其实验室因NIH经费变化而失去研究人员；14%表示移民政策迫使科学家、学生和博士后放弃进入NIH资助实验室的机会。文章将科研经费波动、移民政策和国际研究人才流动联系起来，凸显科研生态对稳定制度环境的依赖。

研判：该条目适合纳入“科技人才与科研资助稳定性”资料组。对上海建设国际科技创新中心而言，科研人才政策不能只强调引进端，还需关注资金连续性、国际人员流动便利性和实验室岗位预期的稳定性；否则高水平科研平台会出现隐性流失。

Fact of the Week: 13 Percent of NIH Researchers Reported That Their Labs Lost Researchers to Other Countries Due to Funding Changes By Meghan Ostertag | June 29, 2026 Source: Andrew Joseph, “Immigration Changes Are Driving Foreign Researchers To Leave the U.S. – Or Not Come to Begin With,” Stat News , May 4, 2026. Commentary: The United States has historically been the most attractive destination for foreign researchers, with the highest levels of R&D investment and many of the world's best hospitals, universities, and research institutions. However, recent changes and upheavals in U.S. science funding and immigration policy have led many prospective foreign researchers to look elsewhere for positions, while many foreign researchers currently based in the United States have left to pursue other opportunities abroad. In a survey of 1,000 U.S. researchers supported by the National Institutes of Health (NIH), 13 percent said that their labs had lost researchers to other countries as a result of NIH funding changes, while 14 percent reported that immigration policies forced scientists, students, and postdocs to turn down offers to work in NIH-supported labs. Moreover, labs have reported receiving fewer applications from international students and researchers, reflecting uncertainty and confusion surrounding the current immigration and research policy environment in the United States.
