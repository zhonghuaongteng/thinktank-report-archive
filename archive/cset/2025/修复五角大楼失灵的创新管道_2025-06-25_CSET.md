# 修复五角大楼失灵的创新管道

## 中文摘要与研判

该页为 CSET 对 Lauren A. Kahn 与 Michael C. Horowitz 在 The National Interest 评论文章的摘要。材料指出，美国国防部沿用的 PPBE 预算流程和国会拨款监督机制，使新兴技术从试验走向部署的过程过于缓慢，难以适应当下技术变化和威慑需求。其核心问题并非缺少技术概念，而是预算、采购、需求确认和规模化采用之间存在制度摩擦。

对科技创新支撑体系研究的启示在于，创新能力取决于从研发、试验、采购到部署的整条管道。对于地方科技政策和上海未来产业布局，该材料可转译为需求侧创新政策议题：如何通过场景开放、首台套采购、任务型预算、快速试点和跨部门资金安排，把新技术从样机和示范项目推向稳定应用。

## English Source Material

CSET’s Lauren A. Kahn and CFR’s Michael C. Horowitz shared their expert analysis in an op-ed published by The National Interest . In their piece, they explore how the U.S. Department of Defense’s outdated budget process is undermining the military’s ability to adopt and scale emerging technologies quickly enough to deter rising global threats. The relationship between the Pentagon and its congressional budget overseers, the appropriations committees, within the Planning, Programming, Budget, and Execution (PPBE) process often turns innovation adoption into a long, grinding battle even in the best of times. The current PPBE process, though better suited to the slower pace of Cold War-era technological change, is now far too rigid and slow for today’s defense needs. Lauren A. Kahn and Michael C.
