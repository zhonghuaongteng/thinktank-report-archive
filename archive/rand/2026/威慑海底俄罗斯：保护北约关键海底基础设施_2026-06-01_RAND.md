# 威慑海底俄罗斯：保护北约关键海底基础设施

## 中文摘要与研判

RAND 页面为评论转载摘要，明确提示余下正文在 War on the Rocks，归档按 summary_only 处理。摘要指出，海底通信电缆、数据链路和能源管线面临快速上升的破坏风险：2023 年 Balticonnector 管道和波罗的海数据电缆受损，2024 年红海四条电缆被切断并扰动亚洲与欧洲之间约四分之一数据流量，2024 年 1 月至 2025 年 7 月约记录 44 起电缆损坏事件。文章将俄罗斯深海情报船、潜艇和影子船队活动作为北约威慑与保护关键海底基础设施的背景。对上海和中国企业的观察意义在于：数字经济安全不只涉及云和数据中心，也包括跨境海底电缆、能源管线和港口周边基础设施韧性，相关安全审查、保险、供应链和国际合作规则会影响全球通信与数据流动。

Deterring Russia Beneath the Waves: Securing NATO’s Critical Undersea Infrastructure Commentary Jun 1, 2026 The USS Bulkeley and a MH-60R Seahawk helicopter assemble in formation for an exercise off the coast of the Shetland Islands, May 12, 2025 Photo by Petty Officer 2nd Class Jacquelin Frost/U.S. Navy By Samu Paukkunen and James Black This commentary was originally published by War on the Rocks on June 1, 2026. What would happen if you woke up one morning and the internet was gone—not from a software glitch, but because someone had simply cut the wire? Threats to critical undersea infrastructure are rapidly escalating. In 2023, the Balticonnector pipeline and several Baltic data cables were damaged. A year later, four Red Sea cables were cut, disrupting a quarter of data traffic between Asia and Europe, with further incidents across the Baltic . In total, between January 2024 and July 2025, roughly 44 incidents of cable damage were recorded. Not all were deliberate, but Russia's activity has grown brazen. In 2025, Moscow sent fighter aircraft to deter Estonian authorities from approaching a shadow fleet vessel near a Poland–Sweden cable.
