# AI智能体基础设施

## 中文摘要与研判

本文提出“智能体基础设施”（agent infrastructure）概念，用以描述位于 AI 智能体外部、能够调节其与现实机构和其他行动者互动的技术系统与共享协议。文章认为，现有对齐技术无法充分解决智能体在法律责任、经济交易、身份识别、服务交互和损害救济中的制度性问题，因此需要面向智能体生态建立外部基础设施。作者概括了三类功能：归属智能体、用户或其他行动者的行为和属性；塑造智能体互动方式；发现并补救智能体造成的损害。

研判要点：该文把 AI 智能体治理从模型内部安全扩展到外部制度接口。对科技治理和数字经济政策而言，关键并不只在于智能体能力本身，还在于认证、授权、责任归属、交易记录、异常处置和跨平台协议能否形成可执行基础设施。上海若推进智能体应用场景和城市级 AI 服务，可将该文作为研究“智能体可信运行环境”的重要参照。

## English Source Material

Infrastructure for AI Agents Increasingly many AI systems can plan and execute interactions in open-ended environments, such as making phone calls or buying online goods. As developers grow the space of tasks that such AI agents can accomplish, we will need tools both to unlock their benefits and manage their risks. Current tools are largely insufficient because they are not designed to shape how agents interact with existing institutions (e.g., legal and economic systems) or actors (e.g., digital service providers, humans, other AI agents). For example, alignment techniques by nature do not assure counterparties that some human will be held accountable when a user instructs an agent to perform an illegal action. To fill this gap, we propose the concept of agent infrastructure: technical systems and shared protocols external to agents that are designed to mediate and influence their interactions with and impacts on their environments. Agent infrastructure comprises both new tools and reconfigurations or extensions of existing tools. For example, to facilitate accountability, protocols that tie users to agents could build upon existing systems for user authentication, such as OpenID. Just as the Internet relies on infrastructure like HTTPS, we argue that agent infrastructure will be similarly indispensable to ecosystems of agents. We identify three functions for agent infrastructure: 1) attributing actions, properties, and other information to specific agents, their users, or other actors; 2) shaping agents' interactions; and 3) detecting and remedying harmful actions from agents. We propose infrastructure that could help achieve each function, explaining use cases, adoption, limitations, and open questions.
