# 面向AI的生物数据是美国下一代战略基础设施

## 中文摘要与研判

文章指出，AI与生物技术的融合将影响全球技术标准、产业供应链、医疗创新、气候韧性和生物防御能力。美国虽持续加大AI投入，但生物数据环境仍然碎片化、投入不足且安全性不充分；若缺乏联邦牵引的AI-ready biodata基础设施，美国可能在AI与生物技术双重竞争中丧失领先位置。

对上海生命健康和生物医药创新体系研究而言，该文的价值在于把数据基础设施视为AI+生物技术产业化的前置条件，而非单纯的科研附属资源。后续可重点跟踪生物数据合规共享、公共数据平台、隐私保护计算、临床数据治理和生物安全之间的政策组合。该CNAS页面为外部刊物入口，页码级引用需回到原刊核验。

## English Source Material

This article was originally published in War on the Rocks . There is little doubt in Washington that AI is a powerful technology that will help determine which country rules the 21st century. Policymakers from the Hill to the White House have made U.S. AI leadership a priority and invested significant resources towards staying ahead of competitors. Yet the United States is at perhaps greater risk than ever before of losing the broader global technology competition. The nation that leads in AI-bio will shape global standards, industrial supply chains, medical innovation, climate resilience, and biodefense. Sustained investment at the scale of tens of billions over a decade is not extraordinary in context. Despite growing investment in AI, U.S. policymakers have failed to prepare for its convergence with biotechnology, a fusion that will define economic and national power in the coming decades. While competitors are building coordinated AI-bio ecosystems, the U.S. biodata (biological data) environment remains fragmented, underfunded, and insecure. Without a federally led effort to build AI-ready biodata as national infrastructure, the United States risks ceding leadership in both AI and biotechnology at a critical moment.
